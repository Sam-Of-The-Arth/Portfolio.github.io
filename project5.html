Eagle Scout Service Project

Engineering & Fabrication

Overview

A structural design and fabrication project involving the creation of four pressure-treated wooden troughs for a working farm. The project included design drafting, material planning, tool coordination, and leading a team of Scouts through on-site construction.

Key Features

  • Hand-drawn design plans with measurements and structural layout
  • Material planning with board sizing, screw selection, and cut lists
  • On-site construction leadership and volunteer coordination
  • Wood cutting, drilling, assembly, and fastening
  • Painting and sealing for weather resistance
  • Full project documentation and workflow planning

Technologies / Tools Used

Pressure-Treated Lumber Power Saws Drill Drivers Exterior Screws Measuring Tools Outdoor Paint & Sealant

Project Details

I designed the troughs on paper, determining dimensions, wood sizing, and screw lengths for structural stability. After gathering materials, I coordinated a team of Scouts on the farm to complete the build. We measured and cut all pieces, assembled the structures using exterior-grade screws, and finished each trough with outdoor paint for durability. The project combined engineering design, fabrication, and project management to deliver long-lasting structures for farm use.
